Preparing For a Wildfire

Preparing an Emergency Plan
- Locating safe exits from home and neighborhood
- Designate a place to meet with family/ other individuals
- Have an emergency contact pickup children should you not have the ability to
- Reach out to people around you and out of town
- Have important info in your possession e.g. health, insurance info
- Know the location of your fire extinguisher, water valve, electrical panel, gas valve, and floor drain
- Have a place for any pets to stay

Emergency Kit
Having an emergency kit is integral, typically when without basic supplies or electricity.
Make sure to gather supplies such as:
- Food (Preferably packed foods that won't spoil)
- Water (4 liters per person/ per day)
- Battery operated/ wind-up flashlight
- Extra clothing & Towels
- Radio
- Knife

Educating The People Around You
Make sure the members of your household are informed:
- Have an emergency plan and emergency kit available to you
- Watch out for weather conditions in your area and look for weather advisories in your area
- Educate yourself on local evacuation routes & procedures and reach out to the local government
- Practice escape route and prepare multiple routes to exit your community

Mitigating Damage to Your Home
It is important to take preventative steps in protecting your home before a wildfire:
- Remove fire hazards such as leaves and branches around your home
- Have a working sprinkler in an accessible position
- Working fire alarms
- Selecting fire resistant materials for the exterior structure of your home
- Leave animals unsheltered

Other Considerations
- Know how to turn off the utilities in your home
- Ensure vehicles have fuel if evacuation needed
- Keep tabs on dependants such as elderly and children
